# Through the Cut — A Panama Canal Story

A 2-player co-op 2D scroller game covering the history of the Panama Canal (1881–1999).

## How to Play

Open `index.html` in a browser. No install needed.

### Controls

| Action       | Player 1 (Rosa) | Player 2 (Thomas) |
|-------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Move         | A / D           | ← / →             |
| Jump         | W               | ↑                 |
| Crouch       | S               | ↓                 |
| Interact     | F               | Enter             |
| Pause        | ESC             | ESC               |

Both players must be present to play. Some puzzles **require both players** to solve.

## Co-op Puzzles

- **Pressure Plates** — Both players stand on them simultaneously
- **Cranks** — Hold interact near a crank to turn it
- **Rope Pulls** — Both players hold interact at opposite rope anchors
- **Levers** — Press interact to pull; sequences must be in the right order

## Characters

- **Rosa** (P1) — Panamanian woman representing local perspective
- **Thomas** (P2) — Caribbean immigrant worker representing the labor force

## Historical Sources

The game covers documented historical events including:
- The French Panama Canal Company (1881–1889) and its 22,000 deaths
- Dr. Carlos Finlay's mosquito discovery (ignored for 20 years)
- Col. Gorgas's mosquito eradication campaign (1904–1906)
- The Gold Roll / Silver Roll racial segregation system (1904–1955)
- The flooding of the Chagres valley displacing 6,000 people (1913)
- The January 9, 1964 Flag Riots (Martyrs' Day)
- The Carter-Torrijos Treaties (1977)
- The December 31, 1999 handover to Panama
